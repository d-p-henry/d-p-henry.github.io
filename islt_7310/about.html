About Me

Hi, my name is Dillon Henry. I'm a library student at the University of Missouri. I also works in a library as a Digital Curation Fellow. I spend a lot of time copying data from removable media, like CDs and floppy disks. In my studies, I care about libraries and justice. I have written about difficulties some communities have with accessing library resources. This includes poorer people, minorities, and prisoners. I'm also interested in technology and libraries. Before studying library science, I studied music. I still enjoy writing songs in my spare time. I also love reading, cooking, hiking, and traveling.

Education credentials:
  • Master’s degree in Library and Information Science at the University of Missouri—Columbia.
  • Master’s degree in Music Composition from the University of Michigan.
  • Bachelor’s degree in Music Composition from Carnegie Mellon University.
  • Minors in Conducting and French & Francophone Studies
