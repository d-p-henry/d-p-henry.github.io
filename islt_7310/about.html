About Me

I am currently completing a master’s degree in Library and Information Science at the University of Missouri—Columbia. Previously, I received a master’s degree in Music Composition from the University of Michigan. I also have a bachelor’s degree in Music Composition from Carnegie Mellon University. Since 2016, I have worked at LaBudde Special Collections & Archives at the University of Missouri—Kansas City. Initially, I worked as an archival assistant. There, I processed collections and created finding aids. After that, I worked briefly as a metadata specialist: I added metadata to photographs for our digital collections. Currently, I’m a Digital Curation Fellow. I am developing policies, procedures, and workflows for preserving digital content on removable media, like CDs and floppy disks.
